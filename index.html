About

I am a second-year graduate student in the division of Pediatric Glioma Research, working in the area of bioinformatics supervised by Dr. David T.W. Jones and Dr. RJ Autry. Before joining as a graduate student, I worked a computational associate at the broad institute in Sellers lab. I did my Masters in Bioinformatics at Georgia Institute of technology was an undergraduate at Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Delhi (IIIT-Delhi) where I earned a major in Computer science and engineering and a minor in Computational biology. My current research focuses on understanding pediatric gliomas, a type of brain tumor in children. I analyze patient data to study how specific proteins and genetic factors interact, which helps us uncover the underlying causes of these tumors. By combining different data modalities, I look at how changes in genes and their regulation may contribute to tumor growth. In the past, I have also worked on identifying health-related genetic variants among populations from Colombia in the context of their ancestry, executed antibody docking and structure prediction for antibodies using modeling software and have processed and analyzed data from CRISPR paralog redundancy screens to identify actionable biomarkers.
