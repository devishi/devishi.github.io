I am a second-year graduate student in the division of Pediatric Glioma Research. I am interested in building models to predict tumorigenesis mechanisms. I completed my Master's in Bioinformatics at Georgia Tech, working on identifying relationships between human genetic ancestry and health in the Colombian population in the lab of King Jordan.

Previously, I have worked at Sellers lab in Broad Institute on identify genetic interactions from combinatorial CRISPR screens as well as a multitude of other projects. I received my Bachelors in Computer Science and Engineering from IIIT Delhi.

Updates

16 September 2024: Gave a talk at the EVEREST symposium about latest insights into identification of super-enhancer based epigenetic regulation in pediatric gliomas
